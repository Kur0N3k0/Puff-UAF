Wind Models

There are several different wind models to choose from:
  • avn - Global prediction model on a 1.25° x 1.25° grid, 1000 to 70 millibars. Forecast data is every six hours to 48 hours, then 60 and 72 hours.
  • badc - Global data set on a 2.5° x 2.5° grid, 1000 to 0.316 millibars, every 12 hours. Archive data is available back to 1990, although all may not be available on this server at this time.
  • nogaps - Global data on a 1.0° x 1.0° grid, 27 levels from 1013 to 10 millibars. Forecast data is every 6 hours out to 144 hours. Nogaps is the highest resolution global data set, so simulations may take quite a while (> 5 min).
  • reanalysis - Global data on a 2.5° x 2.5° grid, 1000 to 10 millibars, every 6 hours. Archive data is available back to ~1950, although all may not be available on this server at this time.
  • WRF - Polar sterographic projection centered on Alaska with a 10 km x 10 km grid, 9 levels from 1000 to 200 millibars. Forecast data is every 3 hours out to 48 hours. Some Outter Aleutian volcanos are not included within this grid.
